📋 7-Day Kickstart Plan

Structure: Daily 500-cal deficit | 3 meals + 1 snack | 10-min workouts | Habit tracking

🥗 Meal Template (Daily)

  • Breakfast (~300 cal): High-protein option (Greek yogurt bowl, eggs + veggies, protein shake)
  • Lunch (~350 cal): Lean protein + vegetables + complex carbs (e.g., chicken salad, quinoa bowl)
  • Dinner (~400 cal): Protein + vegetables + healthy fats (e.g., salmon + roasted veg, tofu stir-fry)
  • Snack (~150 cal): Protein-rich (apple + almond butter, Greek yogurt, cottage cheese)

Total daily target: ~1,200-1,500 cal depending on your BMR (use our calorie calculator)

🏋️ Daily Workout (10 min)

  • Days 1,3,5: Bodyweight circuit — 3 rounds: 30 sec squats, 30 sec modified push-ups, 30 sec glute bridges, 30 sec rest
  • Days 2,4,6: Low-impact cardio — marching in place, step touches, high knees (slow), 10 min continuous
  • Day 7: Active recovery — stretching or 20-min walk

✅ Daily Habit Checklist

  1. ☐ Drink 8 glasses of water
  2. ☐ Eat protein at every meal
  3. ☐ Complete 10-min workout
  4. ☐ Log food intake (paper or app)
  5. ☐ 7+ hours of sleep

🛒 Shopping List (Week 1)

Protein: chicken breast, eggs, Greek yogurt, canned tuna, tofu | Produce: mixed greens, broccoli, bell peppers, berries, lemons, avocado | Pantry: quinoa, rolled oats, olive oil, almonds, chia seeds, spices

Reference: Based on USDA Dietary Guidelines for Americans 2020-2025 and ACSM exercise recommendations. Calorie targets use the Mifflin-St Jeor equation (Frankenfield et al., 2005).

📋 14-Day Challenge Plan

Structure: Daily 400-500 cal deficit | Progressive meals | 15-min workouts | Weekly meal prep | Mindset prompts

🥗 Week 1 Meal Focus: High-Volume Introduction

  • Emphasize vegetables and soups to increase satiety (Rolls, 2005)
  • Protein at 1.4g/kg body weight per day
  • Sample meals from our recipe library

🥗 Week 2 Meal Focus: Habit Reinforcement

  • Add intermittent structure: protein at every meal, fiber 25g+/day
  • Introduce meal prep Sunday: prep 3 lunches + 2 dinners ahead
  • Protein increased to 1.6g/kg body weight

🏋️ Workout Progression

  • Week 1 (Days 1-7): 15 min — Day A: bodyweight circuit, Day B: HIIT (20/10 intervals), Day C: lower body
  • Week 2 (Days 8-14): 15 min — Add 1 extra set to each exercise (increase volume ~20%)
  • Schedule: Workout 5 days/week, active recovery 2 days

💡 Mindset Prompts (Sample)

  • Day 1: "Write one reason why you're doing this."
  • Day 4: "Non-scale victory check — how do your clothes fit?"
  • Day 7: "Review your week. What worked? Adjust and continue."
  • Day 10: "Practice mindful eating — put down your fork between bites."
  • Day 14: "Celebrate 2 weeks! Take measurements, not just weight."

Reference: Habit formation research: Lally et al., How habits are formed, Eur J Soc Psychol (2010) — 66 days average for automaticity. Mindful eating: Katterman et al., Mindfulness meditation and weight loss, Eat Behav (2014).

📋 21-Day Transformation Plan

Structure: Daily 400-500 cal deficit | Weekly themed focus | 20-min progressive workouts | Full habit automation

🗓 Weekly Themes

  • Week 1 (Days 1-7) — Foundation: Establish calorie tracking, daily protein target, and 20-min workout habit. Use calorie and macro calculators.
  • Week 2 (Days 8-14) — Optimization: Fine-tune macros. Add meal prep. Increase workout intensity (progressive overload).
  • Week 3 (Days 15-21) — Automation: Habits should feel automatic. Focus on sleep (7-9 hrs) and stress management. Set maintenance plan.

🥗 Weekly Meal Plans

  • Week 1: Simple meals — grilled protein + roasted vegetables + 1/2 cup whole grain. 3 recipes repeated.
  • Week 2: Add variety — introduce new recipes from our library. Try one new vegetable.
  • Week 3: Meal prep mastery — cook 2x/week in bulk. Freeze portions. Build a go-to recipe roster.

🏋️ Progressive Workouts (20 min each)

  • Week 1: 3 sets x 10-12 reps per exercise, 45 sec rest between sets
  • Week 2: 4 sets x 10-12 reps, 35 sec rest — increase volume by 33%
  • Week 3: 4 sets x 10-12 reps, 30 sec rest — add one compound exercise (e.g., lunges, push-ups)
  • Weekly schedule: 3 strength + 2 HIIT + 2 active recovery

📊 Progress Tracking

  • Weigh yourself daily (same time, morning, after bathroom) — track trend, not daily fluctuations
  • Take measurements (waist, hips, thigh, arm) at Day 1, Day 10, and Day 21
  • Take progress photos in consistent lighting
  • Rate energy level 1-10 daily

Reference: Habit automation: Lally et al. (2010). Progressive overload: Kraemer & Ratamess, Fundamentals of resistance training, Med Sci Sports Exerc (2004). Self-monitoring: Burke et al., Self-monitoring in weight loss, J Am Diet Assoc (2011).
